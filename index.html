Researches ü¶æ

Whole-body Motion Planning and Safety-Critical Control for Aerial Manipulation (arXiv)
  • Accurate whole-body modeling with superquadrics enables precise, differentiable geometry for the aerial manipulator and obstacles.
  • Maximum-clearance planning and safety-critical control combine Voronoi-based trajectory generation with high-order barrier functions for safe, feasible motion.
  • Superior performance to sampling and ellipsoid-based methods, validated in both simulation and real-world experiments.
Planning for quasi-static manipulation tasks via an intrinsic haptic metric: a book insertion case study (IEEE Robotics and Automation Letters)
  • Reframe quasi-static manipulation as a planning problem on an implicit manifold derived from equilibrium conditions.
  • Utilize an intrinsic haptic metric instead of ad-hoc cost functions
  • Propose an adaptive algorithm that simultaneously updates robot states, object positions, contact points, and haptic distances
A Planning Framework for Stable Robust Multi-Contact Manipulation (IROS 2025)
  • Quasi-static mechanical manipulation posed as an optimization problem
  • Parallel scenario optimization for robustness in policy to deal with sensor uncertainty
  • Cost function proposed to encompass: Kinematic target, Friction and squeezing energy, Stability
Path Planning in Complex Environments with Superquadrics and Voronoi-Based Orientation (ROBIO 2025)
  • Combines superquadrics (SQ) with Voronoi diagrams for efficient, differentiable path planning across diverse robot and obstacle geometries.
  • Expands SQ‚Äôs minor axis to eliminate infeasible narrow gaps, aligns motion with Voronoi hyperplanes to maximize clearance and guide orientation.
  • Handles traps and clutter by merging obstacles into Voronoi regions, enabling reliable navigation in 2D & 3D tasks
Recasting Classical Motion Planning for Contact-Rich Manipulation (arXiv)
  • Adapte classical RRT to operate on the quasi-static equilibrium manifold, while deepening the interpretation of haptic obstacles and metrics.
  • Discover multiple manipulation strategies, corresponding to branches of the equilibrium manifold.
  • validating the generality of our method across three diverse manipulation tasks, each requiring only a single manipulation potential expression.
Robotic valve turning: axial misalignment estimation from reaction torques (IROS 2024)
  • Present a simplified quasi-static model of a two-point contact gripper turning a circular valve.
  • Discover geometric features of axial misalignment.
Quasi-static contact-rich manipulation using dynamic movement primitives and haptic potential map (Data-Centric Engineering)
  • Vision-based methods for manipulation struggle with occlusions, while force/torque data is sparse and noisy.
  • Collect interaction force/torque data, build a potential function via Gaussian process regression, and propose Haptic DMPs that integrate force information
  • Demonstrated effective contact-rich manipulation (e.g., peg-in-hole) using only force/torque sensing under quasi-static conditions.
An Energy-Based Numerical Continuation Approach for Quasi-Static Mechanical Manipulation (Data-Centric Engineering)
  • Classical motion planners avoid contact and struggle with manipulation tasks requiring interaction or with objects that cannot be directly controlled..
  • Build on an energy-based quasi-static manipulation framework, introducing a numerical continuation algorithm to compute the equilibrium manifold (EM) via a potential energy formulation.
  • Represent the EM as a graph of equilibria connected by a haptic metric, and validate the framework through an inverted pendulum task, showing effective exploration of contact-rich dynamics..
